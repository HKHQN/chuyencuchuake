Vietnamese Families – Children in the Modern Storm

The family is the cell of society, but today, children face countless pressures: academic demands, social media, generational gaps, domestic violence...

Learn More

How Are Vietnamese Families Changing?

From traditional multi-generational families with many children and strong clan ties → today shifting to nuclear families, fewer children, busy parents, and children growing up with technology. The change brings more freedom and democracy, but also leads to many serious issues, especially for children.

Key Issues Related to CHILDREN

Academic Pressure & Performance Demands

Children are forced into extra classes and intense exams, leading to stress, depression, and even suicidal thoughts. Many study 12–14 hours a day yet still feel "not enough".

Social Media & Online Game Addiction

Children are exposed early to toxic content, cyberbullying, self-comparison, sleep deprivation, and reduced real-life family interaction.

Domestic Violence & Corporal Punishment

Over 70% of children have experienced physical or psychological violence from parents (recent UNICEF & government data show rates around 72% for violent discipline). Long-term effects on psychology and behavior.

Divorce & Parental Separation

Children suffer severe psychological trauma, feeling like "living orphans", easily falling into delinquency or running away from home.

Over-Indulgence or Neglect

One side spoils → children become dependent and selfish; the other side neglects → children lack guidance and easily go astray.

Generational Gap & Lack of Communication

Busy parents, children "living together but strangers", little sharing, leading to misunderstandings and conflicts.

What Can We Do to Protect & Support Our Children?

Parents Lead by Example

Instead of forcing, listen, share, and respect your child's opinions. Spend quality time together every day.

Set Technology Limits

Establish rules for phone/computer use, and explore real-life interests together (sports, arts).

Watch for Psychological Signs

Recognize early signs of depression or anxiety in children (prolonged sadness, behavior changes) → seek professional help if needed.

Build a Warm Family

Eat meals together, talk without judgment, resolve conflicts calmly.
